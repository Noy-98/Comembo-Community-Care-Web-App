About Us

About Us

Barangay Comembo belongs to the Makati City’s second district and is located at the Southeastern of Makati and Northeastern part of Fort Bonifacio. It is part of Cluster 5 or the Eastside Cluster along with East Rembo, Pembo and Rizal. It has a population of 16,818, 3,822 household as of 2015, which are subdivided by eight (8) zonal location and 26 streets with a total land area of 30.90 hectares.Formerly known as Mamancat, then became a home for Combat Engineering Group, which gives birth to Combat Enlisted Men’s Barrio or now known as COMEMBO.

Barangay Comembo Hall

The area now known as Comembo was formerly Mamancat. Even before the coming of the Spanish colonizers, Mamancat was already a vibrant settlement teeming with various economic activities. As its names suggests, Mamancat – from tagalog root word ancat – means import, a central place where good commodities were acquired, purchased or bartered. This activity complemented the role played by its more prosperous neighbor, Aguho (now Pateros) known also during the Spanish time as embarcadero or a place for embarkation. It is an area where goods by foreign merchants coming from as far as India, China and the Middle East were brought and bartered for native goods and commodities.


For more than a hundred years, Mamancat was under the wings of power of Pateros. Its inhabitants continued cultivating its fertile soil. The river, aside from supplying them with food, was also used as their highway to transport their produce, animals, and people going to and from other barangays. It served also as their source of potable water used for drinking, cooking and bathing.


Mamancat was then a part of Meysapan (old name of Makati), a larger settlement, whose forest and mountain fastness served as hunting grounds for fowls, wild plants, fruits and vegetables necessary for the needs of its inhabitants.


When the Secondary World War broke out, Mamancat was totally abandoned by its inhabitants due to fear of being hit by cross forces between the Japanese Imperial Army and the American-Filipino forces combined. Fort William McKinley was an important target for the invading Japanese forces because of its strategic location.


To maintain its presence in the Philippines, the American Colonial Government established military camps in the different parts of the country. It bought four big parcels of real estates from wealthy landowners, titling 850 hectares which lies within the territorial boundaries of Pateros. To the detriment of Mamancat folks, their untitled farmlands were included in the deal. From then on, Mamancat was separated from Pateros and became part of a military reservation called William McKinley.
